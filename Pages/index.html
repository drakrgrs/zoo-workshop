Bears

  1. Ollie
  2. Mona

Bears are large, four-legged mammals that are part of the Ursidae family. They are omnivorous, living on every continent except Antarctica and eating a variety of foods including animals, fruits, and vegetables. Bears are known for being strong, fast, and agile, and can be adept climbers, runners, and swimmers. They also have thick, shaggy coats, non-retractable claws, and short tails.

Giraffes

  1. Frankie
  2. Coconut

Giraffes are the tallest land mammals, with males reaching heights of 18 feet (5.5 meters) or more and females reaching about 4.5 meters. They have long necks and legs, short bodies, tufted tails, short manes, and short, skin-covered horns. Their coats are pale buff with reddish brown spots and a unique pattern.

Lions

  1. Mella
  2. Karl

Lions (Panthera leo) are large cats native to Africa and India that are known as the "king of the beasts". They have muscular bodies with broad chests, short rounded heads, round ears, and dark tufts of hair at the ends of their tails. Male lions are larger than females and have prominent manes of fur around their heads and necks.

Monkeys

  1. Cookie
  2. Earl
  3. Banana Pudding

Monkeys are small to medium-sized primates with long tails that are intelligent, social, and agile. They are part of the infraorder Simiiformes, along with apes and humans, and are divided into two superfamilies: New World monkeys and Old World monkeys

Alligators

  1. Wren
  2. Aspen
  3. Mika

Alligators have a long, rounded snout that has upward facing nostrils at the end; this allows breathing to occur while the rest of the body is underwater. The young have bright yellow stripes on the tail; adults have dark stripes on the tail. It's easy to distinguish an alligator from a crocodile by the teeth.
